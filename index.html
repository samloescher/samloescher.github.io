books

I do a lot of reading, and it largely falls into two categories; Christian and Programming. However I do sometimes venture outside into the world of fiction and enjoyed The Martian recently.

I started programming with Java which I learnt from O'Reilly's Head First Java. It was an excellent book which I would recommend to anyone picking up Java with little prior programming experience. It was through this book I picked up the foundations of Object Oriented design. The most influential programming book I have read since is Clean Code by Robert Cecil Martin. It covers a lot about a lot and sets the reader on an adventure of how to produce better and more maintainable code.

career aspirations

I currently look to be working in cutting edge of technology. Much of what I want to do conversely depends on how to get there, the processes rather than the end product. In as much I find often I prefer to be making things faster rather than initial product design.

I think that I will find myself working in embedded software, a sort of halfway house between electronics and software. The big concern for me however is the use of C++, or rather lack thereof. Much of embedded software today is still written in C, and while C is a very powerful tool there is sadly quite a reluctance to change to C++. Much of the time when I am writing C, I get frustrated at having to waste time dealing with things which the compiler can do for me, or even should be checking for me. The best thing about C++, is that when you let it, it can produce even better results than handwritten C with abstractions which let you focus on the core problem.

hobbies

I enjoy watching programming videos and find myself watching videos outside my expertise so that I get a better appreciation as to why things are done the way they are. Jonathan Blow's videos talking about Jai, a language written for game development have been really interesting. Mike Acton did a superb talk about Data Oriented design which has sent me on a mission to always consider the cache, which most often is the bottleneck.

I absolutely love solving problems, I find often when I have come across a hard problem I will think over it for days on end until I find a solution. Not just any solution mind, I am always striving to find the 'right' solution which I find most aesthetically pleasing, be it the simplest or the one which uses weird and wonderful ways of solving it.
